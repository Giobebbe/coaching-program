Cold Outreach Setup Guide

When to Use Cold Outreach

Cold outreach is for scaling — not for getting your first client.

  • ONLY start cold outreach after your warm network is exhausted (typically Weeks 4-6+)
  • By this point, you should already have: testimonials, at least one case study, and a clear offer
  • Your first clients come from warm outreach (see Warm Outreach Templates)
  • Cold outreach works because you now have proof — you can reference real results from your free clients

If you don't have testimonials yet, go back to warm outreach. Cold email without proof is just spam.

Why We Set Up Infrastructure on Day 1

Here's the catch: domain warmup takes 21+ days.

If you wait until Week 4 to buy domains, you won't be able to send cold emails until Week 7+. That's almost two months wasted.

The move: Buy and warm up your domains from Day 1 while you focus entirely on warm outreach. By the time you've exhausted your warm network and collected testimonials, your cold email infrastructure is ready to go.

Step 1: Buy Your Domains (Day 1)

You need secondary domains — never send cold email from your primary business domain. If your cold emails get flagged as spam, it won't affect your main domain's reputation.

What to buy

  • 1-2 secondary domains that look similar to your primary domain
  • If your primary is youragency.com, buy something like:
  • youragencymail.com
  • youragency.io
  • youragencyhq.com
  • Avoid hyphens, numbers, and anything that looks suspicious

Where to buy

Any of these registrars work:
- Namecheap (cheapest option, easy DNS management)
- Cloudflare (at-cost pricing, great DNS)
- GoDaddy (most popular, slightly more expensive)
- Google Domains (simple if you're already in Google ecosystem)

How many domains

  • Start with 2 domains
  • Each domain can safely send 40-50 cold emails per day once fully warmed
  • 2 domains = 80-100 emails/day capacity at full deployment
  • You can add more domains later as you scale

Set up email accounts on each domain

For each domain, create 2-3 mailboxes. Examples:
- giovanni@youragencymail.com
- g.beggiato@youragencymail.com
- giovanni@youragency.io

Use Google Workspace or Microsoft Outlook for the mailboxes — these have the best deliverability.

Google Workspace setup:
1. Go to workspace.google.com
2. Start a Business Starter plan (~$7/user/month)
3. Add your secondary domain
4. Create the mailboxes listed above

Step 2: Configure Email Authentication (Day 1)

This is the most technical step, but it's critical. Without these three records, your emails will land in spam.

You'll be adding DNS records — text entries in your domain's settings that tell email servers your emails are legitimate.

SPF Record (Sender Policy Framework)

What it does: Tells email servers which systems are allowed to send email on behalf of your domain.

How to set it up:
1. Log into your domain registrar (Namecheap, Cloudflare, etc.)
2. Go to DNS Settings or DNS Management
3. Add a new TXT record with these values:
- Host/Name: @
- Type: TXT
- Value: v=spf1 include:_spf.google.com ~all
- (If using Outlook, the value is different — Google "Microsoft 365 SPF record")
4. Save the record

DKIM Record (DomainKeys Identified Mail)

What it does: Adds a digital signature to your emails that proves they weren't tampered with in transit.

How to set it up:
1. Log into your Google Workspace Admin Console (admin.google.com)
2. Go to Apps > Google Workspace > Gmail
3. Click Authenticate email
4. Click Generate new record — Google will give you a TXT record value
5. Copy that value and add it as a TXT record in your domain's DNS settings
6. Go back to Google Workspace and click Start authentication

DMARC Record (Domain-based Message Authentication)

What it does: Tells receiving email servers what to do with emails that fail SPF or DKIM checks.

How to set it up:
1. In your domain's DNS settings, add a new TXT record:
- Host/Name: _dmarc
- Type: TXT
- Value: v=DMARC1; p=none; rua=mailto:youremail@gmail.com
- (Replace youremail@gmail.com with an email where you want to receive reports)
2. Save the record

Verification

  • After setting up all three records, wait 24-48 hours for DNS propagation
  • Use MXToolbox to verify your records are set up correctly
  • Search for your domain and check SPF, DKIM, and DMARC status
  • If anything shows red, Google "[your registrar] + [the record type] setup guide" — every provider has a walkthrough

Step 3: Set Up Instantly.ai (Day 1)

Instantly is the tool that manages your cold email sending, warmup, and campaigns. It's the industry standard for cold outreach.

Account setup

  1. Go to instantly.ai and create an account
  2. Choose the Growth plan ($30/month) — it covers unlimited email accounts and warmup
  3. Connect your email accounts:
    - Go to Email Accounts in the left sidebar
    - Click Add New and select Google Workspace (or Outlook)
    - Follow the prompts to connect each mailbox you created in Step 1
  4. Enable warmup for every connected account:
    - Click on each email account
    - Toggle Warmup to ON
    - That's it — Instantly handles the rest automatically

Warmup settings

  • Instantly will automatically increase your warmup volume over time
  • You don't need to configure anything manually
  • Keep warmup running forever — even after you start sending real campaigns
  • Warmup works by exchanging emails with other Instantly users' accounts, which builds your sender reputation with email providers

Step 4: The Warmup Timeline

This is your roadmap from Day 1 to full cold email deployment. Follow this exactly.

Days 1-14 (Weeks 1-2): Warmup Only

What's happening What you do
Instantly sends 5-50 warmup emails/day automatically Nothing with cold email. Focus 100% on warm outreach.
Your sender reputation is being established Work your warm network, get free clients, collect testimonials
Warmup emails are being opened, replied to, and marked as "not spam" by other Instantly accounts Set up your lead list (Step 5) so it's ready when you are

Days 15-21 (Week 3): Warmup Only (Still)

What's happening What you do
Warmup volume climbs to 50-90 emails/day Still no cold emails. Your domains aren't ready yet.
Deliverability score is climbing Check your Instantly dashboard — you should see scores improving
Email providers are starting to trust your domain Continue warm outreach. Write your cold email templates (Step 6).

Days 22-30 (Week 4): Start Mixing Cold Emails

What's happening What you do
Domains are now partially warmed Start sending 20-30 cold emails/day per account
Cold emails should be 20-30% of your total daily volume The remaining 70-80% is still warmup
Example: warmup is at 100/day, send 20-30 cold + 70-80 warmup Use your best leads first — don't waste early sends on untested copy

Days 31-60 (Weeks 5-8): Scale Up

What's happening What you do
Domains are well-established Increase to 50-70 cold emails/day per domain
Cold emails = 50-70% of daily capacity Continue warmup in parallel (always)
You should be getting replies and booking calls Monitor: open rates, reply rates, bounce rates (see Step 7)

Days 61-90 (Weeks 9-12): Full Deployment

What's happening What you do
Domains are fully warmed Send 80-100 cold emails/day per domain
Cold emails = 80%+ of daily capacity A/B test subject lines and email copy based on your data
With 2 domains, you're sending 160-200 emails/day Optimize based on what's working

Key rules (non-negotiable)

  • NEVER stop warmup — run it forever alongside your campaigns
  • If bounce rate goes above 3% — reduce volume immediately and clean your list
  • If open rate drops below 27% — check your subject lines and deliverability score
  • Don't increase volume until you're consistently hitting 80%+ inbox placement
  • If in doubt, slow down — it's better to send 30 emails that land in the inbox than 200 that land in spam

Step 5: Building Your Lead List

Your lead list is the single biggest factor in whether cold outreach works. A perfect email sent to the wrong person is worthless.

Where to find leads

LinkedIn Sales Navigator (~$100/month)
- Search by: industry, company size, role, location
- Filter for decision makers at small businesses
- Best for B2B service businesses

Apollo.io (free tier available)
- Email finding + company data
- Can search by company size, industry, tech stack
- Good for building targeted lists with verified emails

Google Maps
- Search for local businesses in specific industries (e.g., "roofing companies in Dallas")
- Great for local service businesses
- Find owners' names, then look them up on LinkedIn or Apollo

What to look for in a lead

  • Small to mid-size businesses (1-50 employees) — these are your ideal clients
  • Businesses with obvious manual processes — if you can see they're doing something by hand that could be automated, that's a lead
  • Industries you've had success with — use what you learned from your warm outreach clients
  • Decision makers: owners, founders, operations managers, marketing managers — the people who can say yes and pay you

List hygiene (this step is critical)

Before you upload any list to Instantly:

  1. Verify every email using a verification tool:
    - NeverBounce — ~$0.003/email
    - ZeroBounce — ~$0.007/email
    - Upload your list, remove any emails that come back as "invalid" or "risky"
  2. Remove duplicates — don't email the same person twice from different accounts
  3. Start small — don't import more than 200-300 leads at a time
  4. Quality over quantity — 200 targeted leads who match your ideal client profile will outperform 2,000 random business owners every time

Step 6: Cold Email Templates

These templates work because they're specific — they reference the recipient's business, industry, or situation. Generic emails get deleted.

Template 1: The Problem-Agitate-Solution

Subject: Quick question about [their company name]

Hi [first name],

I noticed [something specific about their business — e.g., "you're running Facebook ads for your roofing company"].

Most [industry] businesses I've spoken to are losing leads because they can't respond fast enough — especially after hours.

I help businesses like yours [outcome — e.g., "respond to every lead within 60 seconds, 24/7, using AI"] so you never lose a deal to a slower competitor.

Would it make sense to hop on a quick call this week to see if this could work for you?

[Your name]

Why it works: Opens with something specific to them (shows you did research), agitates a real pain point, then offers a clear solution.

Template 2: The Case Study

Subject: How [similar business] [achieved result]

Hi [first name],

I recently helped a [industry] business [specific result — e.g., "cut their lead response time from 4 hours to under 60 seconds"].

They were losing about [X leads per month] because nobody was answering after 5 PM. We set up an AI system that handles it automatically.

I think something similar could work for [their company name]. Open to a quick chat?

[Your name]

Why it works: Leads with proof (your testimonial/case study from warm outreach clients). This is why you needed those results before starting cold outreach.

Template 3: The Direct Question

Subject: [Their company name] + AI

Hi [first name],

Quick question — are you currently using any AI or automation in your business?

I work with [industry] businesses to automate [specific process], and I'd love to see if there's an opportunity to help [their company name].

Worth a 15-minute call?

[Your name]

Why it works: Short, non-threatening, asks a genuine question. Gets replies even from people who aren't sure they need help yet.

Follow-up sequence

Most replies come from follow-ups, not the first email. Set these up as automated follow-ups in Instantly.

Follow-up 1 (3 days after initial email):

Hey [name], just bumping this to the top of your inbox. Would love to chat if you're open to it.

Follow-up 2 (5 days after Follow-up 1):

Hi [name], I know you're busy — just wanted to check if [outcome] is something you'd be interested in. If not, no worries at all.

Follow-up 3 (7 days after Follow-up 2):

Last one from me, [name]. If the timing isn't right, totally understand. But if [problem] is still a challenge, I'd love to help. Just reply "yes" and I'll send over some details.

Rules for follow-ups:
- Keep them short (2-3 sentences max)
- Don't repeat your entire pitch — they already saw it
- The last follow-up should make it easy to say yes ("just reply yes")
- 3 follow-ups is enough. After that, move on.

Step 7: Monitoring & Optimization

What to check daily (takes 5 minutes)

Metric Target If it's off
Bounce rate Under 3% Your list quality is bad — stop sending, verify and clean your list
Open rate 27-44% average, 40-60% is good Rewrite your subject lines or check deliverability score
Reply rate 3-4% average, 5-10% is good Rewrite your email body — your copy isn't resonating
Spam complaints Near 0% Your copy is too aggressive or you're emailing the wrong people

What to check weekly

  • Which subject lines get the highest open rates? Double down on what works.
  • Which email template gets the most replies? Use it more.
  • Are replies positive or negative? If mostly negative, your targeting or copy needs work.
  • Are you booking meetings from replies? If getting replies but not meetings, your call-to-action needs work.

When to hit the pause button

  • Bounce rate above 5%: Stop sending immediately. Clean your entire list with a verification tool before resuming.
  • Deliverability score dropping in Instantly: Reduce cold email volume and increase warmup ratio back to 70-80%.
  • Getting spam complaints: Rewrite your copy — it's too salesy or aggressive. Remove anyone who complains from all future lists.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  1. Starting cold email before you have testimonials/proof — this is the #1 mistake. Cold email without social proof is just noise. Get results from warm outreach first.
  2. Sending 200+ emails/day from a brand new domain — your domain will get blacklisted. Follow the warmup timeline exactly.
  3. Not verifying your email list — one bad list can destroy your domain reputation. Always verify before uploading.
  4. Writing generic copy — "Hi, I help businesses grow" gets deleted. Reference something specific about the recipient's business.
  5. Forgetting to follow up — most responses come from follow-ups 2 and 3, not the first email. Set up automated follow-ups.
  6. Stopping warmup once campaigns start — warmup runs forever. Period.
  7. No clear offer for no clear person with no clear problem — if you can't fill in the blank "I help [specific person] solve [specific problem] by [specific method]," your cold email won't work.
