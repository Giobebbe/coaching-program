Part 2: The Hormozi Value Equation

Your offer needs to score high on this equation:

Value = (Dream Outcome x Perceived Likelihood of Achievement) / (Time Delay x Effort & Sacrifice)

To make your offer more valuable, you can:
- Increase the Dream Outcome (make the result bigger / more desirable)
- Increase Perceived Likelihood (show proof, reduce risk, offer guarantees)
- Decrease Time Delay (deliver results faster)
- Decrease Effort & Sacrifice (make it easy for the client — they don't have to do much)

Part 6: Pricing (For When You Start Charging)

You're NOT pricing from Day 1. First 3 clients are free. But think about this now so you're ready.

Project pricing (one-time fee for building the automation):
- Simple automation (email follow-up, form handling): $1,500-2,500
- Medium complexity (voice agent, multi-step workflow): $3,000-5,000
- Complex system (full business process automation): $5,000-10,000

Retainer pricing (monthly fee for maintaining + supporting the system):
- Formula: 20% of the implementation fee per month
- Example: $2,500 project → $500/month retainer

When to start charging:
- After you have 1-3 testimonials from free clients
- After you've delivered successfully and know what you're doing
- After you have referrals — referred leads trust you more and are easier to close at a paid rate

Reminders

  • You do NOT need a niche on Day 1. Start general. The niche will emerge as you talk to more people.
  • Your offer will evolve. This worksheet is a starting point, not a final answer.
  • Sell the outcome, not the tool. Clients don't care about n8n, Make, or APIs. They care about saving time, making money, and not losing leads.
  • When in doubt, go simpler. One clear solution to one clear problem beats a complicated multi-service offering.
