Part 3: Upwork Research (Do This Around Week 4-6)

Go to Upwork and research whether businesses are already paying for the type of work you've been doing. This validates demand.

Search terms to try:
- "AI automation [industry]"
- "Make.com [industry]"
- "n8n automation"
- "AI chatbot for [industry]"
- "lead generation automation"
- "CRM automation"
- "[industry] automation"

What to look for:

Search Term # of Jobs Posted Average Budget Frequency (new jobs/week)

What this tells you:
- Lots of jobs posted = high demand
- High budgets = businesses are willing to pay well for this
- Frequent new posts = ongoing demand (not a one-time trend)
- Few jobs = either low demand OR an untapped opportunity (dig deeper)

Part 5: Niche Decision Framework

When to niche down:
- You've had 5+ conversations with businesses in the same industry
- You've delivered at least 1-2 projects in that space
- You see consistent demand (from outreach responses + Upwork research)
- You enjoy working with these clients

When NOT to niche down yet:
- You've only talked to a handful of people
- No clear pattern has emerged
- You're still in your first 3-4 weeks of outreach

How to niche — by INDUSTRY, not by solution:
- Good: "I help restaurants automate their operations with AI"
- Good: "I help real estate agencies never miss a lead using AI"
- Less good (too early): "I build n8n voice agents" (this is a solution, not a niche)

Your niche statement (when ready):

"I help [industry] businesses [solve what problem] using AI so they can [outcome]."

Reminders

  • The niche is not a permanent decision. It's a focus area that you can adjust as you learn more.
  • Don't force it. If nothing is clear by Week 6, keep going general and pay attention to what comes.
  • The transcript is clear: your first few clients will probably come from random industries because you're asking your warm network "do you know anyone?" That's fine. The niche comes after.
  • Niching by industry (not solution) means your cold email, LinkedIn outreach, and messaging become sharper: you can reference specific problems that specific businesses face.
