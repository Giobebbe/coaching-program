Delivery Framework Template

This covers Steps 3-6 of the delivery process (after onboarding). For Steps 1-2, see the Client Onboarding Checklist.

Step 3: Weekly Progress Updates

Send an update every week on the same day. It takes 2 minutes and makes a massive difference in how the client perceives your work.

Why this matters: If the client doesn't hear from you, they don't know if you're building their system or watching Netflix. Weekly updates increase their confidence that they made a good investment.

Weekly Update Template

Hey [client name], just wanted to give you a quick update.

This week: We finished [milestone — e.g., setting up all the integrations and connecting your CRM].

Next week: We're moving into [next milestone — e.g., building the core automation logic].

Everything is on track. [Optional: mention something specific — "The lead capture form is already pulling data correctly, which is great."]

I'll update you again next [day]. Let me know if you have any questions in the meantime.

Rules:
- Send it even if there's not much progress — "Still working on [milestone], ran into a small issue with [X] but working through it" is better than silence
- Keep it short — 3-5 sentences max
- Always mention what's coming next — it keeps momentum
- If something is delayed, be upfront about it immediately. Don't wait until the deadline.

Step 4: Testing

Do NOT hand off anything you haven't thoroughly tested. If the client finds bugs, you've destroyed the trust you built.

Testing Checklist

End-to-End Testing:
- Run the entire automation from start to finish
- Verify every step produces the correct output
- Check that data flows correctly between all integrations
- Confirm the final output matches what was promised

Edge Case Testing:
- What happens if someone fills out a form incorrectly?
- What happens if an API call fails or times out?
- What happens with unexpected input (empty fields, special characters, very long text)?
- What happens if two triggers fire at the same time?
- For AI agents: what happens when the AI doesn't understand the question?

Stress Testing:
- Run the automation at least 5-10 times (20-30 if it involves AI)
- Check for consistency — does it produce the same result every time?
- Test at different times of day (if timing matters)

Integration Testing:
- All connected tools still working (APIs, CRM, email, etc.)
- Notifications/alerts going to the right places
- Data being stored/logged correctly

If something doesn't work → fix it before delivery. No exceptions.

Step 5: Documentation

You deliver two things: a video walkthrough and a written guide. This makes you look professional and makes the client self-sufficient.

Loom Video Walkthrough

Record a screen-share video walking through the automation step by step.

Important: Make the video GENERIC so the client's team can use it. Don't say "Hey John, great working with you." Instead say:

"Hey, the purpose of this video is to act as documentation for [Company Name]'s team. I'm going to walk you through how this automation works and how to use it step by step."

What to cover in the video:
1. What the automation does (the outcome, in plain English)
2. How it works, step by step (show the flow)
3. How to make changes if needed (e.g., update a response, change an email template)
4. What to do if something goes wrong (who to contact, basic troubleshooting)

Keep it under 10 minutes if possible. Record with Loom (free plan works).

Written SOP (Standard Operating Procedure)

Turn the Loom video into a written document.

Quick method: Take the Loom transcript → paste into ChatGPT or Claude → ask:
"Turn this transcript into a step-by-step SOP document with clear headers, numbered steps, and any relevant screenshots described."

SOP Template:

[Automation Name] — Standard Operating Procedure

Overview

This document explains how [automation name] works and how to use it.

What It Does

[1-2 sentences explaining the outcome in plain English]

How It Works

  1. [Step 1 — what triggers the automation]
  2. [Step 2 — what happens next]
  3. [Step 3 — and so on]
    ...

How to Make Changes

  • To update [X]: go to [location] and change [field]
  • To add [Y]: [instructions]

Troubleshooting

  • If [problem]: try [solution]
  • If [problem]: try [solution]
  • For anything else: contact [your name] at [your email/phone]

Step 6: Handoff + Maximizing Client Value

Delivery Email Template

Send this email when the project is complete and ready to hand off:

Subject: [Project Name] is Complete and Ready to Go

Hey [name],

I'm excited to let you know that [project name] is complete and ready to use.

Just so you and your team know exactly how everything works, I've put together two things for you:

  1. Video walkthrough showing you exactly how the automation works and how to use it: [Loom link]
  2. Written step-by-step guide with all the details: [Google Doc / PDF link]

Everything is set up, tested, and ready to go live. If you have any questions or need any tweaks, just let me know.

[If paid — final invoice:]
I've also submitted the final invoice for the remaining 50% of the project. You can pay that here: [payment link]

Thanks again for trusting me with this project. I had a great time working on it.

Talk soon,
[Your name]

PS — I noticed while working on this project that there are probably other areas in your business where AI could help. I'd love to offer you a free business audit where we can map out other processes and identify opportunities to implement AI. No obligation — just want to make sure you're getting the most value possible. Let me know if you'd like to hop on a quick call to discuss this.

Why the PS Upsell Works

  • You've just delivered — trust is at its highest
  • You're offering something free (a business audit)
  • You're planting a seed for more business without being pushy
  • Not every client will take you up on it, but the ones who do become your best clients

Asking for Referrals

After delivery (or during the handoff call):

"Hey [name], I'm really glad [project/automation] is working well for you. As we discussed at the start, I'd love to ask — do you know two other people who might benefit from something similar? A quick intro would mean the world to me."

Remember: 91% of people say they'd give referrals, but only 11% are ever asked. Just ask.

Retainer Proposal

If the project lends itself to ongoing maintenance:

"Hey [name], now that [project] is live, I wanted to mention something. Many of my clients find it valuable to have ongoing support — making sure the system runs smoothly, optimizing based on real data, and handling any issues. I offer a monthly maintenance plan for $[amount]/month, which covers support, updates, and optimization. Would that be useful for you?"

Retainer pricing: 20% of the implementation fee per month
- $2,500 project → $500/month
- $5,000 project → $1,000/month

The Big Picture: Why Delivery Matters for Your Business

From the transcript: "The difference between an agency that's struggling and one that's successful isn't how many clients you take on. It's how much value you extract from each client."

  • 1 client who pays you $10K over 10 months > 10 clients who pay you $1K each
  • Great delivery → referrals → more clients without more outreach
  • Great delivery → upsells → more revenue without finding new clients
  • Great delivery → retainers → predictable monthly income

The best clients are the ones you've solved the most problems for. Don't chase 100 new clients — go deeper with the ones you have.
